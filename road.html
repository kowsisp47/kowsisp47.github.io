Project Overview

This project is a deep learning system that automatically detects and classifies road surface defects — potholes and cracks — directly from road images. Given an input photo, the model identifies whether a defect is present, determines what type it is, draws a bounding box around its exact location in the image, and assigns a severity level of low, moderate, or high based on the extent of the damage.

I built this to explore a real infrastructure problem: road damage assessment is still largely done by manual inspection, which is slow, inconsistent, and doesn't scale across large municipal road networks. A CNN-based detection pipeline can process images far faster than a human inspector while producing consistent, repeatable severity ratings.

It's aimed at municipal road maintenance teams, civic tech applications, and researchers exploring automated infrastructure monitoring — anyone who needs a fast, visual, and quantifiable way to triage which roads need repair first.

Problem Statement

Municipal road networks are typically inspected on foot or by slow-moving survey vehicles, with defects logged manually by an inspector's judgment. This process is time-consuming, expensive to scale, and highly subjective — two inspectors looking at the same pothole may rate its severity differently.

Because inspection is slow, damage often goes unreported for long stretches of time, allowing minor cracks to worsen into large potholes before repair crews are even aware of them. Without a consistent, quantifiable severity scale, maintenance teams also struggle to prioritize which roads need urgent attention versus routine upkeep.

An automated, image-based detection system solves both problems at once: it can process large volumes of road imagery quickly, and because the severity classification is model-driven rather than subjective, every road gets rated on the same consistent scale.

Implementation

01

Preprocessing Module

Resizes, normalizes, and denoises incoming road images using OpenCV so the model receives consistent input regardless of source resolution or lighting.

02

Feature Extraction Module

A convolutional neural network learns visual patterns distinguishing cracks, potholes, and healthy road surface from the preprocessed image.

03

Detection Module

A YOLO-based detection head localizes each defect in the image and outputs a bounding box with a class label for every instance found.

04

Severity Classification Module

Evaluates each detected defect's size and shape characteristics to assign a Low, Moderate, or High severity rating.

05

Visualization Module

Renders the final bounding boxes and severity labels directly onto the original image for easy visual interpretation.

Challenges Faced

🎯 Detection Accuracy

Distinguishing shallow cracks from shadows or surface staining required careful tuning of the CNN's feature layers.

📊 Severity Calibration

Defining a consistent, defensible boundary between Low, Moderate, and High severity took multiple rounds of testing against labeled examples.

🖼️ Bounding Box Precision

Getting the YOLO detection head to tightly localize irregular pothole shapes, rather than oversized or misaligned boxes, required extra training iterations.

🌦️ Varying Image Conditions

Road photos taken in different lighting, angles, and weather needed a robust preprocessing step to keep accuracy consistent.
