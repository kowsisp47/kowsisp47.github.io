Project Overview

Mediction AI is a full-stack medical reporting platform built to help doctors generate structured, professional patient reports without typing a single word. A doctor simply speaks their observations aloud, and the system handles the rest — converting speech to text, correcting medical terminology, structuring the content into a proper report format, and generating a downloadable PDF ready for a patient's file.

The project was born out of a simple observation: clinicians spend a disproportionate amount of their time on documentation rather than patient care. I built Mediction AI to explore how speech recognition and generative AI could be combined to remove that friction, while still producing output that is accurate and clinically usable rather than a rough transcript.

It's designed for individual practitioners, small clinics, and telemedicine providers who need a fast, reliable way to turn a verbal consultation into a formal record — without investing in expensive enterprise medical software.

Problem Statement

Manual medical reporting is one of the most time-consuming parts of a clinician's day. Doctors typically type or dictate notes after every consultation, a process that eats into time that could otherwise go toward patient care. Multiply this across dozens of patients a day, and documentation quickly becomes a significant operational bottleneck.

Beyond the time cost, manual entry introduces real risk of human error — misspelled medical terminology, inconsistent formatting, and missing details are common when notes are written under time pressure. Report data is also often scattered across paper files or disconnected systems, making patient history difficult to retrieve when it matters most.

These problems point to a clear need for automation: a system that can capture a doctor's spoken input accurately, correct it against real medical vocabulary, and produce a consistent, structured report automatically — which is exactly the gap Mediction AI was built to close.

Implementation

01

Speech Module

Captures live audio from the doctor and converts it into raw text using a speech recognition pipeline, handling background noise and varying speaking pace.

02

Correction Module

Passes the raw transcript through a medical terminology correction layer to fix misheard drug names, conditions, and clinical terms before they reach the report.

03

AI Module

Sends the corrected text to Gemini AI with a structured prompt, returning a formatted report with the appropriate medical report sections.

04

Database Module

Persists patient records and report history in SQLite, keyed so previous visits can be retrieved instantly during a follow-up.

05

Report Module

Formats the final AI output into a clean document layout and renders it as a downloadable PDF for the patient's file.

Challenges Faced

🩺 Medical Terminology Correction

Generic speech recognition frequently mis-transcribed clinical terms, requiring a dedicated correction layer trained on medical vocabulary.

🤖 AI Integration

Getting Gemini AI to consistently return well-structured, clinically appropriate report formatting took significant prompt iteration.

🗄️ Database Management

Designing a schema that could cleanly link patients, visits, and reports while staying simple enough for a lightweight SQLite setup.

🎙️ Speech Accuracy

Background noise and natural speech pauses during consultations affected transcription quality and needed additional handling.
